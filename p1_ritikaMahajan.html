Water Efficiency and Scarcity

Saving every drop matters- here's how we can make a real difference together

How water scarcity affects your life

Water scarcity isn't just a distant future or something happening far away. It can affect the amount of water you have available at home, how clean that water is and how much it costs. This is a real possibility as the climate shifts and the population grows even for countries we think as "water-safe" today

when water becomes limited, it affects households, farms, enerygy production, hygiene and public health. To sum it up, water scarcity is everyone's problem and its essential for each one of us to make efficient use of water.

Why is the water scarcity rising?

A heavy pressure is put on water supplies by the evergrowing population, expantion of cities and the rising demand from industries and agriculture. Climate change making things worse as it results in droughts and unpredictable rainfall patterns.

According to global data, 4 billion people experience sevear water scarcity at least one month per year.

The facts thats worse still is that today, about 2.2 billion people still lack safely managed drinking water services.

What does water efficiency really mean?

Water efficiency is about using water in a conservative manner. Its about taking action researching and implimenting smarter tools (water-saving devices), better habits (fixing leaks, turning off taps all the way) and long term strategies (recycling water, harvesting rain).

Its about making every drop count.

Everyday behaviours that drain more water than you think...

Small everyday actions add up. Long showers, leaving the water running while brushing teeth, leaving leaks unfixed- now imagine that on a larger scale across millions of households.

Your actions matter. Being aware of that fact and spreading that awareness and the gravity of the situation is what will help us move towards a future that is water-efficient.

Small Changes Make Big Impacts

Home

Home efficiency upgrades

  • Install low flow showerhead and taps
  • Install low volume or dual flush toilets
  • Use water efficient dishwashers and washing machines

Behaviour

everyday behavious

  • Take shorter showers
  • Turn off taps while brushing teeth
  • Fix small leaks immediatly

Gardening

water efficient gardening

  • Water plants at cooler hours of the day
  • Store rainwater to use for gardening
  • Check if the plant even needs water by checking the moistness of the soil

Broader Solutions

Community level solutions

  • Promote rainwater harvesting
  • Implimenting greywater harvesting
  • Raise awareness though campaigns

Innovation and technology

New tools make water conservation easier. Technologies like leak detectors, water efficient appliances and modern plumbing systems make a huge difference.
